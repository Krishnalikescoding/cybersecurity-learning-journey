# Course 1 - Module 2 Key Terms and Definitions

This document contains important cybersecurity terms introduced in Course 1, Module 2.

---

## Adversarial Artificial Intelligence (AI)

A technique that manipulates Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) systems to conduct attacks more effectively and efficiently.

---

## Business Email Compromise (BEC)

A type of phishing attack where a threat actor impersonates a trusted source to gain a financial advantage.

---

## CISSP

**Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)** is a globally recognized cybersecurity certification awarded by the **International Information Systems Security Certification Consortium (ISC²)**.

---

## Computer Virus

Malicious code designed to interfere with computer operations and damage data, programs, or systems.

---

## Cryptographic Attack

An attack that targets secure communications and encryption mechanisms between a sender and intended recipient.

---

## Hacker

Any person who uses computers to gain access to computer systems, networks, or data.

---

## Malware

Software intentionally designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to devices, systems, or networks.

### Examples

* Viruses
* Worms
* Trojans
* Ransomware
* Spyware

---

## Password Attack

An attempt to gain unauthorized access to password-protected devices, systems, networks, or data.

### Examples

* Brute Force Attack
* Rainbow Table Attack

---

## Phishing

The use of digital communication to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information or installing malicious software.

---

## Physical Attack

A security incident that affects both physical and digital environments.

### Examples

* Malicious USB Devices
* Card Skimming
* Card Cloning

---

## Physical Social Engineering

An attack in which a threat actor impersonates an employee, customer, or vendor to gain unauthorized physical access to a location.

---

## Social Engineering

A manipulation technique that exploits human error to gain private information, access, or valuables.

---

## Social Media Phishing

A phishing attack where a threat actor gathers information about a target through social media before launching an attack.

---

## Spear Phishing

A targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific individual or group while appearing to come from a trusted source.

---

## Supply-Chain Attack

An attack that targets systems, applications, hardware, or software through vulnerabilities in third-party suppliers or vendors.

---

## USB Baiting

An attack in which a threat actor leaves an infected USB device for someone to find and connect to a computer, unknowingly spreading malware.

---

## Virus

Another term for a **Computer Virus**, which is malicious code designed to damage systems or data.

---

## Vishing

Voice phishing that uses phone calls or voice communication to obtain sensitive information or impersonate a trusted source.

---

## Watering Hole Attack

An attack where a threat actor compromises a website frequently visited by a targeted group of users.

## Key Takeaways

* Social engineering attacks exploit human behavior rather than technical vulnerabilities.
* Malware includes many different forms of malicious software, including viruses.
* Phishing attacks have several variants such as spear phishing, vishing, BEC, and social media phishing.
* Supply-chain attacks can impact multiple organizations through trusted third parties.
* Understanding these terms is essential for identifying and responding to common cybersecurity threats.
