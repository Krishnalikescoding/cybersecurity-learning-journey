    # Common Cybersecurity Attack Types

Cybersecurity professionals must understand various attack methods used by threat actors. Different attack types target systems, networks, data, and even human behavior. Many of these attacks align with one or more of the eight CISSP security domains.

---

## 1. Password Attacks

A password attack is an attempt to gain unauthorized access to password-protected systems, devices, networks, or data.

### Common Types

* Brute Force Attack
* Rainbow Table Attack

### Related CISSP Domain

**Communication and Network Security**

### Example

An attacker repeatedly tries different password combinations until the correct password is discovered.

---

## 2. Social Engineering Attacks

Social engineering is a manipulation technique that exploits human error to gain access to information, systems, or valuable resources.

### Common Types

* Phishing
* Smishing (SMS phishing)
* Vishing (Voice phishing)
* Spear Phishing
* Whaling
* Social Media Phishing
* Business Email Compromise (BEC)
* Watering Hole Attack
* USB Baiting
* Physical Social Engineering

### Related CISSP Domain

**Security and Risk Management**

### Example

A threat actor sends a fake email that appears to come from a trusted organization to steal login credentials.

---

## 3. Physical Attacks

A physical attack targets hardware, devices, or physical environments to compromise security.

### Common Types

* Malicious USB Cable
* Malicious Flash Drive
* Card Cloning
* Card Skimming

### Related CISSP Domain

**Asset Security**

### Example

An attacker leaves an infected USB drive in a public location hoping an employee plugs it into a company computer.

---

## 4. Adversarial Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Adversarial AI involves manipulating artificial intelligence and machine learning systems to improve attack effectiveness or bypass security controls.

### Related CISSP Domains

* Communication and Network Security
* Identity and Access Management (IAM)

### Example

Attackers manipulate AI models to evade malware detection systems.

---

## 5. Supply-Chain Attacks

A supply-chain attack targets vendors, suppliers, software providers, or other third parties to compromise systems before they reach the final customer.

### Characteristics

* Exploits vulnerabilities in trusted third-party components
* Can impact multiple organizations simultaneously
* Often difficult to detect

### Related CISSP Domains

* Security and Risk Management
* Security Architecture and Engineering
* Security Operations

### Example

Malware is inserted into a software update distributed to thousands of organizations.

---

## 6. Cryptographic Attacks

A cryptographic attack targets encryption methods and secure communications to gain access to protected information.

### Common Types

* Birthday Attack
* Collision Attack
* Downgrade Attack

### Related CISSP Domain

**Communication and Network Security**

### Example

An attacker exploits weaknesses in an encryption algorithm to decrypt sensitive information.

---

---

## Key Takeaways

* Cyberattacks can target technology, people, processes, and physical assets.
* A single attack may affect multiple CISSP security domains.
* Understanding attack types helps security professionals identify risks and implement effective defenses.
* Security awareness and proactive security controls are essential for reducing the likelihood of successful attacks.
