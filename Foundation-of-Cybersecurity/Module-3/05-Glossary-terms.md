# Module 3: Terms and Definitions

## Asset
An item perceived as having value to an organization.

## Availability
The idea that data is accessible to authorized users when needed.

## Compliance
The process of following internal standards and external regulations.

## Confidentiality
The principle that only authorized users can access specific assets or data.

## CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
A security model that helps organizations manage risk by ensuring:
- **Confidentiality** – Only authorized users can access data.
- **Integrity** – Data remains accurate and trustworthy.
- **Availability** – Data is accessible when needed.

## Hacktivist
A person who uses hacking to achieve political or social goals.

## HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
A U.S. federal law that protects patients' health information.

## Integrity
The principle that data is accurate, authentic, and reliable.

## NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
A voluntary framework of standards, guidelines, and best practices for managing cybersecurity risk.

## Privacy Protection
The practice of safeguarding personal information from unauthorized access or misuse.

## Protected Health Information (PHI)
Information related to an individual's past, present, or future physical or mental health.

## Security Architecture
A security design made up of tools, technologies, and processes used to protect an organization from threats.

## Security Controls
Safeguards implemented to reduce specific security risks.

## Security Ethics
Guidelines that help security professionals make legal and ethical decisions.

## Security Frameworks
Structured guidelines used to manage security risks and protect data and privacy.

## Security Governance
Practices that define, direct, and support an organization's security efforts.

## Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)
A type of personally identifiable information (PII) that requires stricter protection due to its sensitive nature.
