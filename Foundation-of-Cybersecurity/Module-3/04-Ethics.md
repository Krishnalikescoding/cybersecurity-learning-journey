# Security Ethics

Security ethics are guidelines for making appropriate decisions as a cybersecurity professional. Ethical behavior requires remaining unbiased and protecting the security and confidentiality of private data.

## Ethical Concerns and Counterattacks

### United States Standpoint
- Counterattacks are **illegal** for private organizations and individuals.
- Relevant laws:
  - Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (1986)
  - Cybersecurity Information Sharing Act (2015)
- Security professionals can **only defend**, not attack.
- Counterattacks are considered **vigilantism**.
- Risks of counterattacks:
  - Escalate attacks
  - Cause more damage
  - Create international conflicts if state-sponsored hackers are involved
- Only authorized **U.S. federal government** and **military personnel** may conduct counterattacks.

### International Standpoint
According to the **International Court of Justice (ICJ)**, a counterattack is only acceptable if it:
1. Targets only the original attacker.
2. Is a direct request for the attacker to stop.
3. Does not escalate the conflict.
4. Can be reversed.

> **Note:** Most organizations avoid counterattacks because these conditions are difficult to guarantee.

# Ethical Principles

## Confidentiality
- Only authorized users should access data.
- Security professionals must respect privacy and protect sensitive information.

## Privacy Protection
Protect personal information from unauthorized access.

### Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Information that can identify a person, such as:
- Name
- Phone number
- Address

### Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)
More sensitive data requiring stricter protection, including:
- Social Security Numbers
- Credit Card Numbers
- Other highly sensitive personal information

### Ethical Responsibilities
Security professionals should:
- Protect private data
- Identify vulnerabilities
- Manage organizational risks
- Align security with business goals

# Laws and Ethics

Laws are rules enforced by governments. Security professionals must:

- Remain unbiased
- Work honestly and responsibly
- Respect all applicable laws
- Base decisions on evidence
- Be transparent and fair
- Stay committed to protecting organizations
- Continuously improve cybersecurity skills

---

# HIPAA Example

**HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)** is a U.S. law that protects **Protected Health Information (PHI)**.

Requirements:
- Patient health information cannot be shared without consent.
- Organizations must notify affected patients if their healthcare data is breached.
- Security professionals help ensure legal and ethical compliance.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Ethics guide cybersecurity decision-making.
- Counterattacks are generally illegal and discouraged.
- Protect confidentiality, privacy, and sensitive data.
- Follow laws and professional ethical standards.
- Continuously improve skills to strengthen cybersecurity.
