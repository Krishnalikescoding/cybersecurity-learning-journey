# Cybersecurity Key Terms and Definitions

This document contains important cybersecurity terms introduced in Course 1, Module 1.

---

## Cybersecurity (Security)

The practice of ensuring **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA)** of information by protecting networks, devices, people, and data from unauthorized access or criminal exploitation.

---

## Cloud Security

The process of ensuring that assets stored in the cloud are properly configured and accessible only to authorized users.

---

## Internal Threat

A current or former employee, external vendor, or trusted partner who poses a security risk to an organization.

---

## Network Security

The practice of protecting an organization's network infrastructure from unauthorized access, misuse, or attacks.

---

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

Any information that can be used to identify or infer the identity of an individual.

### Examples

* Full Name
* Email Address
* Phone Number
* Home Address

---

## Security Posture

An organization's ability to defend critical assets and data while adapting to changes in the threat landscape.

---

## Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)

A type of PII that requires stricter protection and handling due to its sensitive nature.

### Examples

* Social Security Number
* Passport Number
* Financial Information
* Medical Records

---

## Technical Skills

Skills that require knowledge of specific tools, technologies, procedures, and security policies.

### Examples

* Network Analysis
* Programming
* Vulnerability Assessment
* Security Monitoring

---

## Threat

Any circumstance, event, or action that can negatively impact an organization's assets, systems, or data.

---

## Threat Actor

Any individual or group that presents a security risk to an organization.

### Examples

* Cybercriminals
* Hacktivists
* Insider Threats
* Nation-State Attackers

---

## Transferable Skills

Skills gained from other experiences or professions that can be applied to cybersecurity roles.

### Examples

* Communication
* Problem-Solving
* Critical Thinking
* Teamwork
* Time Management

---

## Key Takeaways

* Cybersecurity focuses on protecting information and systems from threats.
* PII and SPII require careful handling to protect user privacy.
* Threat actors can originate from both inside and outside an organization.
* Strong technical and transferable skills are essential for cybersecurity professionals.
* A strong security posture helps organizations defend against evolving threats.
