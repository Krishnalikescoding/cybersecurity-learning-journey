# Cybersecurity Skills

Cybersecurity professionals rely on both **transferable skills** and **technical skills** to be successful.

## Transferable Skills

Transferable skills are skills gained from other experiences that can be applied across different careers.

### Communication
- Collaborate with team members and stakeholders.
- Understand questions and concerns from others.
- Communicate technical information clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences.
- Help resolve security issues efficiently through effective communication.

### Problem-Solving
- Identify and analyze security threats and vulnerabilities.
- Recognize attack patterns and security risks.
- Determine effective solutions to minimize risk.
- Be willing to try new approaches and make compromises when necessary.

### Time Management
- Prioritize tasks based on urgency and impact.
- Respond quickly to security incidents.
- Stay focused on the most critical issues.
- Help reduce damage to organizational assets and data.

### Growth Mindset
- Continuously learn new technologies and security practices.
- Adapt to the rapidly changing cybersecurity landscape.
- Apply new knowledge to professional development and security operations.

### Diverse Perspectives
- Respect different viewpoints and experiences.
- Encourage teamwork and collaboration.
- Use diverse perspectives to develop stronger security solutions.

---

## Technical Skills

Technical skills require knowledge of specific tools, technologies, and procedures used in cybersecurity.

### Programming Languages
- Automate repetitive tasks.
- Search and analyze large amounts of data.
- Identify potential threats and attack patterns.
- Organize and process security-related information efficiently.

### Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)
- Collect and analyze log data.
- Monitor security events and activities.
- Detect unusual behavior and potential threats.
- Improve the efficiency of threat detection and investigation.

### Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
- Monitor systems and networks for suspicious activity.
- Detect possible intrusions and unauthorized access attempts.
- Generate alerts when malicious activity is detected.
- Help protect organizational assets and data.

### Threat Landscape Knowledge
- Stay informed about:
  - Threat actors
  - Malware
  - Attack techniques and methodologies
- Recognize emerging threats and new attack trends.
- Build stronger defenses against cyberattacks.

### Incident Response
- Follow established security policies and procedures.
- Respond appropriately to security incidents.
- Investigate alerts and determine root causes.
- Contain, remediate, and recover from security events.

## Key Takeaways

### Transferable Skills
- Communication
- Problem-Solving
- Time Management
- Growth Mindset
- Diverse Perspectives

### Technical Skills
- Programming Languages
- SIEM Tools
- Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
- Threat Landscape Knowledge
- Incident Response

### Why These Skills Matter
A successful cybersecurity analyst combines strong interpersonal and analytical abilities with technical expertise to identify threats, protect systems, and respond effectively to security incidents.
