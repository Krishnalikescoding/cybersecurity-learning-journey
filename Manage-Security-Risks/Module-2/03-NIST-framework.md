# Module 2: NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

## NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)

**NIST** is a U.S.-based organization that develops cybersecurity standards, guidelines, and best practices used worldwide to improve security.

Organizations use NIST frameworks to:

- Manage cybersecurity risks
- Protect sensitive data and assets
- Build security policies
- Improve overall security posture

---

# NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

The **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** is a **voluntary framework** consisting of standards, guidelines, and best practices to help organizations manage cybersecurity risk.

It can be used by:

- Businesses
- Government agencies
- Non-profit organizations
- Organizations of any size

### Benefits

- Improves security posture
- Reduces cybersecurity risk
- Helps detect and respond to incidents
- Supports business continuity
- Protects critical assets and data

---

# NIST CSF v2.0 Core Functions

The latest version (**CSF v2.0**) includes **six core functions**:

## 1. Govern *(New in CSF v2.0)*

Establish cybersecurity governance by:

- Defining security policies
- Assigning roles and responsibilities
- Managing cybersecurity risks
- Supporting organizational decision-making

---

## 2. Identify

Understand and manage organizational assets, risks, and vulnerabilities.

Examples:

- Inventory devices
- Identify critical assets
- Assess cybersecurity risks
- Understand business environment

---

## 3. Protect

Implement safeguards to reduce security risks.

Examples:

- Access controls
- Encryption
- Employee awareness training
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

---

## 4. Detect

Identify cybersecurity events as quickly as possible.

Examples:

- Security monitoring
- Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
- Log analysis
- SIEM tools

---

## 5. Respond

Take action after detecting a security incident.

Examples:

- Investigate incidents
- Contain threats
- Notify stakeholders
- Perform incident response

---

## 6. Recover

Restore systems and normal operations after an incident.

Examples:

- Restore backups
- Recover lost data
- Repair affected systems
- Improve future security measures

---

# Workplace Example

A workstation is reported as compromised.

1. **Govern** – Follow organizational cybersecurity policies and incident response procedures.
2. **Identify** – Determine which workstation is affected and discover an unknown USB device.
3. **Protect** – Block the unknown device to prevent further damage.
4. **Detect** – Analyze logs and monitor systems for additional malicious activity.
5. **Respond** – Investigate the incident and determine the source of the attack.
6. **Recover** – Restore affected files, repair the workstation, and return it to normal operation.

**Root Cause:** An employee connected an infected phone to their work laptop through a USB port.

# NIST SP 800-53

**NIST Special Publication (SP) 800-53** provides a catalog of security and privacy controls for U.S. federal information systems and organizations.

### Purpose

- Protect federal information systems
- Maintain the CIA Triad
- Standardize security controls
- Improve risk management

It is especially important for organizations that work with the **U.S. federal government**.

---

# CSF v2.0 Updates

### New "Govern" Function

CSF v2.0 introduces **Govern**, highlighting the importance of:

- Cybersecurity leadership
- Risk governance
- Policy management
- Defined security roles and responsibilities

### Supply Chain Risk Management

The updated framework places greater emphasis on:

- Third-party vendor security
- Supply chain risk management
- Managing risks from external partners and suppliers

---

# Key Takeaways

- **NIST** develops globally recognized cybersecurity standards and best practices.
- The **NIST CSF** is a voluntary framework for managing cybersecurity risk.
- **CSF v2.0** includes **six functions**:
  1. Govern
  2. Identify
  3. Protect
  4. Detect
  5. Respond
  6. Recover
- The framework helps organizations prevent, detect, respond to, and recover from cyber incidents.
- **NIST SP 800-53** provides security controls for U.S. federal information systems.
- CSF v2.0 places additional focus on **cybersecurity governance** and **supply chain risk management**.
