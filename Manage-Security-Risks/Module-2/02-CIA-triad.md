# Module 2: CIA Triad in the Workplace

## CIA Triad

The **CIA Triad** is a security model that helps organizations manage risk and build strong security policies. It consists of three core principles:

- **Confidentiality**
- **Integrity**
- **Availability**

Following the CIA Triad helps organizations maintain a strong **security posture**, which is their ability to protect critical assets, manage risks, and respond to security threats.

---

# 1. Confidentiality

**Definition:**
Only authorized users should have access to sensitive information.

### Purpose
- Protect private data
- Prevent unauthorized access
- Reduce data exposure

### How it's achieved
- Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)
- Access controls
- Authentication
- Authorization
- Encryption

### Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)

Users are given **only the minimum access** needed to perform their job.

**Example:**
- An HR employee can access employee records but not financial databases.

---

# 2. Integrity

**Definition:**
Data should remain accurate, authentic, reliable, and unaltered.

### Purpose
- Prevent unauthorized modification
- Ensure trustworthiness of data
- Detect tampering

### How it's achieved
- Cryptography
- Encryption
- Hashing
- Digital signatures
- Integrity checks

### Cryptography

Transforms data into a secure format so unauthorized users cannot read or modify it.

### Encryption

Converts **plaintext** into **ciphertext** to prevent unauthorized access or tampering.

**Example:**
Messages on an organization's internal communication platform are encrypted to protect them from modification.

---

# 3. Availability

**Definition:**
Authorized users should be able to access data and systems whenever needed.

### Purpose
- Ensure continuous access
- Minimize downtime
- Support business operations

### How it's achieved
- Backups
- Disaster recovery plans
- Redundant systems
- Regular maintenance
- Network monitoring

**Example:**
Remote employees securely access the organization's internal network to perform their work.

Access is still controlled based on job responsibilities.

Example:
- Accounting employees access financial records.
- Developers access project repositories.
- Employees cannot access information unrelated to their role.

---

# Security Posture

**Security Posture** is an organization's ability to:

- Protect critical assets
- Manage cybersecurity risks
- Respond to changing threats
- Maintain confidentiality, integrity, and availability

A strong security posture is built by applying the CIA Triad effectively.

---

# Key Takeaways

- The **CIA Triad** consists of:
  - Confidentiality
  - Integrity
  - Availability
- **Confidentiality** ensures only authorized users can access data.
- **Integrity** ensures data remains accurate, authentic, and unaltered.
- **Availability** ensures authorized users can access systems and data when needed.
- The **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)** limits users to the minimum permissions required.
- **Cryptography** and **encryption** help protect confidentiality and integrity.
- The CIA Triad is the foundation of an organization's **security posture** and cybersecurity strategy.
