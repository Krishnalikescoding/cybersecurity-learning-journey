# Ransomware and the Impact of Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities

## Ransomware

**Ransomware** is a type of malware in which threat actors encrypt an organization's data and demand payment (a ransom) in exchange for a **decryption key** to restore access.

### Effects of a Ransomware Attack
- Encrypts (locks) confidential data
- Freezes network systems
- Makes devices unusable
- Disrupts normal business operations
- Demands payment before restoring access

**Decryption Key:** A key or password used to decrypt encrypted data and regain access.

---

# Layers of the Web

## Surface Web
- Publicly accessible through standard web browsers.
- Includes websites like search engines, news sites, and online stores.

## Deep Web
- Requires authorization or login to access.
- Example: An organization's intranet or internal database.

## Dark Web
- Accessible only through special software.
- Provides anonymity and is often used for illegal activities such as selling stolen data or ransomware negotiations.

---

# Impacts of Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities

## 1. Financial Impact

Cyberattacks can result in significant financial losses due to:
- Business interruptions
- Loss of productivity
- Recovery and remediation costs
- Regulatory fines for non-compliance
- Loss of revenue

---

## 2. Identity Theft

Organizations often store sensitive information such as:
- Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
- Employee records
- Customer data
- Vendor information

If compromised, this data may be stolen, leaked, or sold on the dark web, leading to identity theft and privacy violations.

---

## 3. Reputational Damage

Security incidents can damage an organization's reputation by:
- Reducing customer trust
- Losing existing customers
- Driving customers to competitors
- Generating negative publicity
- Resulting in legal penalties and fines

---

# Reducing Risk

Organizations should:
- Implement strong security controls
- Follow security policies and compliance requirements
- Prepare for incidents using response plans
- Use security tools to detect and respond to threats
- Train employees on cybersecurity awareness

Proper preparation helps organizations minimize the impact of attacks such as ransomware.

---

# Key Takeaways

- **Ransomware** encrypts data and demands payment for a decryption key.
- The web consists of three layers: **Surface Web**, **Deep Web**, and **Dark Web**.
- Threats, risks, and vulnerabilities can lead to:
  - Financial losses
  - Identity theft
  - Reputational damage
- Strong security measures and incident response plans help reduce the impact of cyberattacks.
