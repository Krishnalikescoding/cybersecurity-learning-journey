# Course 2 - Module 1: Terms and Definitions

## Assess
The **fifth step** of the **NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)** that determines whether established security controls are implemented correctly and operating effectively.

## Authorize
The **sixth step** of the **NIST RMF** in which an organization accepts accountability for the security and privacy risks associated with a system.

## Business Continuity
An organization's ability to continue normal business operations during and after a disruption by using disaster recovery and continuity plans.

## Categorize
The **second step** of the **NIST RMF** used to classify systems and develop appropriate risk management processes and tasks.

## External Threat
Any threat originating outside the organization that has the potential to harm organizational assets.

## Implement
The **fourth step** of the **NIST RMF** that involves deploying security and privacy controls throughout the organization.

## Internal Threat
A current or former employee, contractor, vendor, or trusted partner who poses a security risk to the organization.

## Monitor
The **seventh step** of the **NIST RMF** that involves continuously monitoring systems and security controls to ensure they remain effective.

## Prepare
The **first step** of the **NIST RMF** that focuses on activities necessary to manage security and privacy risks before a security incident occurs.

## Ransomware
A type of malware that encrypts an organization's data and demands payment in exchange for a decryption key to restore access.

## Risk
Anything that can impact the **Confidentiality, Integrity, or Availability (CIA)** of an asset.

## Risk Mitigation
The process of implementing policies, procedures, and security controls to reduce the likelihood or impact of a risk.

## Security Posture
An organization's overall ability to protect its assets, manage risks, and respond to changes in the threat landscape.

## Select
The **third step** of the **NIST RMF** that involves selecting, customizing, and documenting the security controls used to protect an organization.

## Shared Responsibility
The principle that everyone within an organization plays an active role in reducing risk and maintaining both physical and digital security.

## Social Engineering
A manipulation technique that exploits human behavior to gain unauthorized access, sensitive information, or valuables.

## Vulnerability
A weakness in a system, process, or control that can be exploited by a threat actor.
