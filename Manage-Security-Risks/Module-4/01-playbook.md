# Playbooks and Incident Response

## What is a Playbook?

A **playbook** is a documented guide that provides **predefined, step-by-step instructions** for responding to operational or security incidents.

It ensures that every security team member follows a **consistent, efficient, and compliant** response process.

---

# Why Playbooks are Important

Playbooks help organizations:

- Respond quickly to incidents
- Standardize response procedures
- Reduce errors
- Ensure legal and regulatory compliance
- Improve teamwork and coordination
- Minimize damage from security incidents
- Maintain business continuity

---

# Components of a Playbook

### Strategy

Defines:

- Goals
- Team expectations
- Roles and responsibilities

### Plan

Specifies:

- Tasks to perform
- Order of actions
- Required tools
- Response procedures

---

# Living Documents

Playbooks are **living documents**, meaning they are regularly reviewed and updated.

Updates are made when:

- Security process failures are discovered
- Industry standards or regulations change
- New cyber threats emerge
- Threat actor tactics evolve
- Lessons are learned from previous incidents

---

# Types of Playbooks

Organizations create playbooks for different purposes, including:

- Incident response
- Vulnerability response
- Security alerts
- Team-specific procedures
- Product-specific procedures
- Ransomware attacks
- Business Email Compromise (BEC)
- Vishing
- Phishing and other attack scenarios

---

# Incident Response

### Definition

**Incident response** is the process of identifying, containing, eliminating, and recovering from a cybersecurity incident while minimizing its impact.

---

# Incident Response Playbook Phases

## 1. Preparation

### Goal

Prepare the organization before an incident occurs.

### Activities

- Create incident response plans
- Define team roles and responsibilities
- Train employees
- Document procedures
- Prepare tools and resources

---

## 2. Detection and Analysis

### Goal

Identify and understand security incidents.

### Activities

- Monitor SIEM alerts
- Analyze logs
- Determine whether an incident occurred
- Assess scope and severity
- Identify affected systems

---

## 3. Containment

### Goal

Limit the spread and impact of the incident.

### Activities

- Isolate affected systems
- Block malicious IP addresses
- Disable compromised accounts
- Prevent further damage

---

## 4. Eradication and Recovery

### Goal

Remove the threat and restore normal operations.

### Eradication

- Remove malware
- Eliminate malicious files
- Fix vulnerabilities

### Recovery

- Restore systems
- Verify security
- Resume normal business operations
- Perform IT restoration

---

## 5. Post-Incident Activities

### Goal

Learn from the incident and improve future responses.

### Activities

- Document the incident
- Report findings to leadership
- Perform root cause analysis
- Update policies and procedures
- Improve security controls
- Update the playbook

---

## 6. Coordination

### Goal

Ensure effective communication throughout the incident response process.

### Activities

- Share information among teams
- Report incidents
- Meet compliance requirements
- Coordinate internal and external responses

---

---

# Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

### Definition

A **Business Continuity Plan (BCP)** outlines how an organization will continue operating during and after a disruption, such as a cyberattack.

Incident response and vulnerability playbooks are developed to support the organization's BCP.

---

# Risk Formula

A simple way to think about risk:

**Risk = Likelihood of a Threat × Potential Impact**

Higher likelihood and greater impact result in higher organizational risk.

---

# SIEM and Playbooks

SIEM tools and playbooks work together.

### SIEM

- Detects threats
- Collects and analyzes logs
- Generates alerts

### Playbook

- Guides the response
- Defines required actions
- Ensures consistency
- Supports compliance

**Example Workflow**

1. SIEM detects suspicious activity.
2. An alert is generated.
3. Security analyst opens the appropriate playbook.
4. The analyst follows each response phase.
5. Incident is contained, eradicated, and documented.

# Key Takeaways

- A **playbook** is a step-by-step guide for responding to operational and security incidents.
- Playbooks include a **strategy** (roles and expectations) and a **plan** (specific actions and procedures).
- They are **living documents** that are updated as threats, technologies, regulations, and organizational processes evolve.
- The **six phases of an incident response playbook** are:
  1. Preparation
  2. Detection and Analysis
  3. Containment
  4. Eradication and Recovery
  5. Post-Incident Activities
  6. Coordination
- **Business Continuity Plans (BCPs)** help organizations continue operating during and after disruptions, and incident response playbooks support these plans.
- **SIEM tools** detect and alert on security events, while **playbooks** provide structured guidance for responding effectively and consistently.
- Regularly reviewing incidents and updating playbooks helps organizations strengthen their overall **security posture**.
