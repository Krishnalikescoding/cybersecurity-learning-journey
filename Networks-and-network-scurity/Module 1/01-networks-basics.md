# Network Basics

## What is a Network?

A **network** is a group of connected devices that communicate and share data or resources.

### Examples of Network Devices

- Computers
- Laptops
- Smartphones
- Tablets
- Servers
- Printers
- Smart TVs
- IoT devices (e.g., smart refrigerators, cameras)

Devices can communicate through:

- **Wired connections** (Ethernet cables)
- **Wireless connections** (Wi-Fi)

---

# Network Addresses

To communicate correctly, every device uses unique identifiers.

## IP Address (Internet Protocol Address)

- Identifies a device on a network.
- Used to send and receive data between devices.
- Can be **IPv4** or **IPv6**.

**Example:** `192.168.1.10`

---

## MAC Address (Media Access Control Address)

- A unique hardware address assigned to a network interface card (NIC).
- Used for communication within a **Local Area Network (LAN)**.
- Usually permanent and assigned by the manufacturer.

**Example:** `00:1A:2B:3C:4D:5E`

---

# Types of Networks

## 1. Local Area Network (LAN)

### Definition

A **LAN (Local Area Network)** connects devices within a **small geographic area**.

### Examples

- Home Wi-Fi
- School network
- Office network
- College campus network

### Characteristics

- Small coverage area
- High-speed communication
- Privately managed
- Connects local devices

---

## 2. Wide Area Network (WAN)

### Definition

A **WAN (Wide Area Network)** connects multiple LANs across **large geographic areas**.

### Examples

- The Internet
- Corporate branch offices
- International business networks

### Characteristics

- Covers cities, countries, or continents
- Connects remote locations
- Uses internet service providers (ISPs)
- Enables long-distance communication

---

# LAN vs WAN

| Feature | LAN | WAN |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Full Form | Local Area Network | Wide Area Network |
| Coverage | Small area (home, school, office) | Large area (city, country, world) |
| Speed | Generally faster | Generally slower than LAN |
| Ownership | Private | Multiple organizations/ISPs |
| Example | Home Wi-Fi | Internet |

---

# How Devices Communicate

1. Devices connect to a LAN (wired or wireless).
2. Each device is assigned an **IP address**.
3. Each network interface has a unique **MAC address**.
4. Devices locate each other using these addresses.
5. Data is exchanged over the LAN or across a WAN (such as the Internet).

---

# Real-World Example

At home:

- Your phone, laptop, and smart TV connect to your **Wi-Fi (LAN)**.
- The home router connects the LAN to the **Internet (WAN)**.
- Through the Internet, you can communicate with users and services anywhere in the world.

---

# Key Takeaways

- A **network** is a collection of connected devices that communicate and share resources.
- Devices communicate using **wired (Ethernet)** or **wireless (Wi-Fi)** connections.
- Every device has a unique **IP address** for network communication and a **MAC address** that identifies its network hardware.
- A **LAN (Local Area Network)** connects devices within a small area such as a home, school, or office.
- A **WAN (Wide Area Network)** connects multiple LANs across large geographic areas; the **Internet** is the largest example of a WAN.
- LANs allow local communication, while WANs enable communication between networks across cities, countries, and the world.
